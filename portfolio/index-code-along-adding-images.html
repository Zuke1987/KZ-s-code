About Me

I have some experience with programming in Java and Python through coursework taken at the university. I have also taken some other computer science courses such as a course in discrete math. In addition, I have read a few books on the Java language, HTML, and CSS as well as completed some tutorials in Python, and MySQL. Nevertheless, I still consider my self a beginner when it comes to software and web development. My background is in two fields that are seemingly unrelated to the development world but both have given me skills that would make me a successful developer.

I graduated in 2005 with a degree in Linguistics and a TESOL teaching certificate, and recently graduated in May of this year with a degree in Nursing. Both degrees and subsequent jobs that I have been employed in have given me people skills, critical thinking skills, the ability to problem solve, as well as various other skills which are applicable to a developer. Aside from academic background I also possess character traits that would make me a successful developer. I am a self-learner, persistent, competitive, hard-working, analytical, logical, and I have an innate drive to succeed and laser focus when there is something I want to accomplish.

Past Experiences

There are many accomplishments that I am proud of but a few that really stand out and that required a high degree of consistency and commitment to accomplish. One accomplishment I am extremely proud of is having moved to Japan and having lived and worked there. This required me to face my fear of the unknown and to survive and thrive in a foreign country that has a vastly different culture and language relative to the western world. Every day was a challenge and I constantly had to adapt in order to acclimate myself to a new world. Instead of quitting and going home I stayed there for over three years and was able to thrive. While there, I also achieved one of my long time goals. I was able to pass the second highest Japanese proficiency test. This required me to be consistent in my study of Japanese over the course of three years, and I spent countless hours studying to pass this exam. Having taken it three times before passing, I also demonstrated my relentess "never quit" attitude in order to pass.

One other accomplishment I am especially proud of is graduating from nursing school. This required an unwavering level of commitment to study daily for hours and hours and to never quit despite some "failures." It pushed me beyond my comfort level both physically and emotionally and allowed me to grow as an individual. I was able to get a 4.0 in the prerequisite classes usually required for medical school, which I once feared and considered to be extremely difficult. I demonstrated to myself that I can accomplish anything I put my mind to and it gave me the confidence to pursue any future so called "impossible" endeavors.

Hobbies

I have several hobbies but if I had to mention just few I would say my favorites are studying foreign languages, and playing instruments like the guitar and piano. As for studying languages, it gives me great satisfaction learning new words and ways of expressing ideas in a new language, and communicating with others who have such a different perspective on life. I also enjoy trying to discern the patterns in language so that I can express myself effectively.

I also enjoy playing the guitar and piano. It allows me a creative outlet to express what cannot be conveyed in words. I also feel a great sense of accomplishment when I finish learning an entire song. It is just an amazing experience to connect with something that is deep within yourself but also something separate from yourself.
